# Malaria

## Overview

Malaria is a serious and potentially life-threatening infectious disease caused by *Plasmodium* parasites. It is transmitted to humans through the bite of infected female *Anopheles* mosquitoes. Malaria remains one of the most common diseases in many tropical and subtropical countries, including Nigeria.

Early diagnosis and prompt treatment greatly improve recovery and reduce the risk of severe illness and death.

---

## Also Known As

- Malaria
- Plasmodium infection

---

## Causes

Malaria is caused by parasites of the *Plasmodium* species. The most common species affecting humans are:

- Plasmodium falciparum
- Plasmodium vivax
- Plasmodium ovale
- Plasmodium malariae
- Plasmodium knowlesi

The infection usually spreads through the bite of an infected female *Anopheles* mosquito.

Less common methods of transmission include:

- Blood transfusion from an infected donor
- Organ transplantation
- Sharing contaminated needles
- Mother-to-child transmission during pregnancy or childbirth

---

## Risk Factors

The following increase the risk of malaria:

- Living in or travelling to malaria-endemic areas
- Sleeping without insecticide-treated mosquito nets
- Stagnant water around homes
- Poor environmental sanitation
- Pregnancy
- Children under five years
- Older adults
- People with weakened immune systems

---

## Signs and Symptoms

Symptoms usually appear between 7 and 30 days after an infected mosquito bite.

Common symptoms include:

- Fever
- Chills
- Sweating
- Headache
- Muscle pain
- Joint pain
- Weakness
- Fatigue
- Loss of appetite
- Nausea
- Vomiting
- Mild diarrhoea

---

## Severe Malaria Symptoms

Severe malaria is a medical emergency.

Seek immediate medical care if any of the following occur:

- Difficulty breathing
- Persistent vomiting
- Confusion
- Convulsions or seizures
- Loss of consciousness
- Severe weakness
- Yellow eyes or skin (jaundice)
- Dark-coloured urine
- Severe anaemia

---

## Possible Complications

Without prompt treatment, malaria can lead to:

- Cerebral malaria
- Kidney failure
- Severe anaemia
- Respiratory distress
- Liver damage
- Low blood sugar
- Shock
- Death

---

## Diagnosis

Healthcare professionals may diagnose malaria using:

- Rapid Diagnostic Test (RDT)
- Blood smear microscopy
- Laboratory blood tests when necessary

Diagnosis should always be confirmed before treatment whenever possible.

---

## Treatment

Treatment depends on:

- The type of malaria
- Severity of illness
- Age
- Pregnancy status
- Local treatment guidelines

Most uncomplicated malaria is treated with Artemisinin-based Combination Therapy (ACT).

Severe malaria requires urgent hospital treatment with injectable antimalarial medicines and supportive care.

Do not take antimalarial medicines without professional medical advice.

Always complete the full course of prescribed treatment.

---

## Home Care

If malaria has been diagnosed and treatment has started:

- Take all medicines exactly as prescribed.
- Drink plenty of clean fluids.
- Rest adequately.
- Eat light, nutritious meals.
- Monitor body temperature.
- Return to the hospital if symptoms worsen.

---

## Prevention

Malaria can often be prevented by:

- Sleeping under insecticide-treated mosquito nets every night.
- Removing stagnant water around homes.
- Keeping surroundings clean.
- Using mosquito repellents.
- Wearing long-sleeved clothing during evenings.
- Installing window and door screens.
- Seeking medical attention promptly when fever develops.

---

## Lifestyle Advice

Protecting yourself from mosquito bites is the most effective way to reduce malaria risk.

Families should:

- Maintain clean surroundings.
- Cover water containers.
- Clear blocked drains.
- Participate in community environmental sanitation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can malaria spread from one person to another?

No. Malaria does not spread through casual contact such as touching, hugging, or sharing food. It is mainly transmitted through infected mosquito bites.

### Can malaria be cured?

Yes. Most people recover completely when malaria is diagnosed early and treated correctly.

### Can malaria occur more than once?

Yes. Previous infection does not provide lifelong protection. A person can become infected multiple times.

### Should everyone with fever assume it is malaria?

No. Fever can be caused by many illnesses. Proper medical testing is recommended before treatment.

---

## When to Seek Emergency Care

Go to the nearest hospital immediately if the patient develops:

- Difficulty breathing
- Convulsions
- Persistent vomiting
- Confusion
- Unconsciousness
- Severe weakness
- Inability to drink fluids
- Very high fever that does not improve
- Signs of dehydration

---

## Key Takeaways

- Malaria is caused by Plasmodium parasites.
- It is transmitted by infected female Anopheles mosquitoes.
- Early diagnosis improves recovery.
- Prompt treatment reduces complications.
- Sleeping under treated mosquito nets is one of the best preventive measures.
- Severe malaria requires immediate hospital treatment.
