# First Aid

## Overview

First aid is the immediate care provided to a person who becomes injured or suddenly ill before professional medical treatment is available. The primary goals of first aid are to preserve life, prevent the condition from worsening, promote recovery, and ensure the person's safety until healthcare professionals arrive.

First aid does **not** replace professional medical treatment. It is intended to provide immediate assistance while waiting for emergency medical services or transport to a healthcare facility.

---

# Goals of First Aid

The main goals of first aid are to:

- Preserve life
- Prevent further injury
- Promote recovery
- Reduce pain where possible
- Reassure the injured person
- Arrange prompt medical care

---

# The DRSABCD Approach

A useful emergency response sequence is **DRSABCD**.

## D – Danger

Check that the area is safe for yourself, the injured person, and others before approaching.

Do not place yourself in danger.

---

## R – Response

Check whether the person responds by speaking to them and gently tapping their shoulder.

If there is no response, call for help immediately.

---

## S – Send for Help

Call your local emergency medical service or ask someone nearby to do so.

Provide:

- Exact location
- Nature of the emergency
- Number of injured persons
- Your contact number

---

## A – Airway

Check whether the airway is open.

If necessary and there is no suspected neck injury, gently open the airway using an appropriate technique.

Remove only visible obstructions. Do not blindly sweep inside the mouth.

---

## B – Breathing

Look for normal breathing.

If the person is not breathing normally, begin cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) if you are trained and it is safe to do so.

---

## C – Circulation

Look for signs of severe bleeding.

Control life-threatening bleeding immediately using firm direct pressure.

---

## D – Defibrillation

If an Automated External Defibrillator (AED) is available, follow its voice instructions while continuing CPR until emergency responders arrive.

---

# Personal Safety

Before providing first aid:

- Ensure the environment is safe
- Wear disposable gloves if available
- Avoid direct contact with blood or body fluids
- Wash your hands after providing care
- Dispose of contaminated materials safely

---

# First Aid Kit

A well-equipped first aid kit may include:

- Adhesive bandages
- Sterile gauze
- Medical tape
- Elastic bandage
- Antiseptic solution
- Disposable gloves
- Scissors
- Tweezers
- Digital thermometer
- Instant cold pack
- Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS)
- CPR face shield (if available)
- Flashlight
- Emergency contact list

Check supplies regularly and replace expired items.

---

# Wound Care

## Steps

- Wash your hands
- Stop bleeding with gentle pressure
- Clean the wound with clean running water
- Remove visible dirt carefully
- Apply antiseptic if recommended
- Cover with a sterile dressing
- Change dressing regularly

Seek medical care for deep, contaminated, or infected wounds.

---

# Controlling Bleeding

- Apply firm direct pressure
- Use clean cloth or dressing
- Keep pressure until bleeding stops
- Elevate limb if appropriate
- Seek urgent care if bleeding is severe

Do not remove embedded objects.

---

# Nosebleeds

- Sit upright and lean forward
- Pinch soft part of nose for 10–15 minutes
- Breathe through mouth
- Avoid blowing nose immediately

Seek medical help if bleeding persists.

---

# Minor Burns

- Cool under running water for at least 20 minutes
- Remove jewelry if not stuck
- Cover with non-stick dressing

Do NOT:

- Apply butter or oil
- Break blisters

---

# Fractures

## Signs

- Severe pain
- Swelling
- Deformity
- Loss of movement

## First Aid

- Immobilize area
- Apply cold pack
- Seek urgent care

Do not realign bones.

---

# Sprains and Strains (RICE Method)

- Rest
- Ice
- Compression
- Elevation

Seek medical care if severe.

---

# Dislocations

- Immobilize joint
- Apply cold pack
- Seek urgent care

Do not attempt repositioning.

---

# Choking

If coughing effectively, encourage it.

If unable to breathe or speak:

- Call emergency services immediately
- Provide trained first aid if available

If unconscious:

- Begin CPR if trained

---

# CPR Overview

:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0} is used when breathing stops or heart function is absent.

- Call emergency services
- Start chest compressions
- Use AED if available
- Continue until help arrives

---

# Recovery Position

If breathing normally but unconscious:

- Place on side
- Keep airway open
- Monitor continuously

---

# Poisoning

- Remove from source safely
- Call poison control or emergency services
- Keep container if possible

Do not induce vomiting unless instructed.

---

# Animal Bites

- Wash wound with soap and water for 15 minutes
- Cover with clean dressing
- Seek medical care

May require tetanus or rabies treatment.

---

# Snake Bites

- Keep person calm
- Immobilize limb
- Remove tight items
- Seek emergency care

Do NOT cut or suck wound.

---

# Heat Illness

## Heat Exhaustion

- Move to cool place
- Give fluids if conscious
- Cool body

## Heat Stroke (Emergency)

- Call emergency services
- Cool immediately

---

# Fainting

- Lay flat
- Elevate legs
- Loosen clothing
- Monitor breathing

---

# Seizures

- Protect from injury
- Do not restrain
- Do not place objects in mouth
- Time seizure

Seek help if >5 minutes.

---

# Eye Injuries

- Do not rub eye
- Rinse with clean water if needed
- Cover lightly
- Seek urgent care

Do not remove embedded objects.

---

# Electric Shock

- Switch off power source first
- Call emergency services
- Check breathing
- Start CPR if needed
