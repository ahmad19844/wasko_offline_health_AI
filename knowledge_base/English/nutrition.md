# Nutrition

## Overview

Nutrition is the process by which the body obtains and uses nutrients from food for growth, energy, repair, and maintaining good health. A healthy diet provides the right balance of nutrients needed for proper body function and helps prevent many diseases.

Good nutrition supports physical growth, brain development, strong immunity, healthy pregnancy, and recovery from illness. Poor nutrition, on the other hand, increases the risk of infections, chronic diseases, and nutritional deficiencies.

Healthy eating should begin in infancy and continue throughout life.

---

# Importance of Good Nutrition

Good nutrition helps:

- Maintain a healthy body weight
- Support growth and development
- Build strong bones and muscles
- Improve brain function
- Strengthen the immune system
- Reduce the risk of chronic diseases
- Improve wound healing
- Support healthy pregnancy
- Increase energy levels
- Promote healthy aging

# Nutrients

Nutrients are substances in food that the body needs to function properly.

The six major nutrient groups are:

- Carbohydrates
- Proteins
- Fats
- Vitamins
- Minerals
- Water

Each nutrient performs important functions.

---

# Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are the body's main source of energy.

Healthy carbohydrate sources include:

- Rice
- Millet
- Sorghum
- Maize
- Oats
- Sweet potatoes
- Irish potatoes
- Yam
- Whole wheat
- Beans
- Fruits

Choose whole grains whenever possible because they contain more fibre.

---

# Proteins

Proteins are essential for:

- Growth
- Tissue repair
- Muscle development
- Enzyme production
- Hormone production
- Healthy immune function

Healthy protein sources include:

- Fish
- Chicken
- Lean beef
- Eggs
- Beans
- Lentils
- Soybeans
- Milk
- Yogurt
- Groundnuts

Children, pregnant women, and recovering patients require adequate protein intake.

---

# Healthy Fats

Fats provide energy and help the body absorb vitamins A, D, E, and K.

Healthy fats include:

- Avocados
- Groundnuts
- Almonds
- Walnuts
- Sesame seeds
- Sunflower seeds
- Olive oil
- Vegetable oils
- Fatty fish

Limit foods high in saturated fats and avoid trans fats whenever possible.

---

# Water

Water is essential for life.

Water helps:

- Regulate body temperature
- Transport nutrients
- Remove waste products
- Lubricate joints
- Support digestion
- Maintain healthy skin

People should drink adequate clean water daily. Water needs increase during hot weather, illness, pregnancy, breastfeeding, and physical activity.

# Vitamins

Vitamins are essential nutrients required in small amounts to support normal body functions, growth, immunity, and disease prevention.

## Vitamin A

Vitamin A supports:

- Healthy eyesight
- Strong immunity
- Healthy skin
- Normal growth

Sources include:

- Carrots
- Mangoes
- Sweet potatoes
- Pumpkin
- Liver
- Eggs
- Dark green leafy vegetables

A deficiency may cause:

- Night blindness
- Frequent infections
- Poor growth

---

# Malnutrition

Malnutrition occurs when the body does not receive the right amount of nutrients.

It includes:

- Undernutrition
- Overnutrition
- Micronutrient deficiencies

Malnutrition can affect people of all ages.

---

# Undernutrition

Undernutrition occurs when a person does not consume enough nutrients.

Causes include:

- Poverty
- Food insecurity
- Illness
- Poor feeding practices
- Chronic disease

Symptoms may include:

- Weight loss
- Poor growth
- Weakness
- Frequent infections
- Delayed development in children

---

# Overnutrition

Overnutrition occurs when a person consumes more calories than the body requires.

This increases the risk of:

- Obesity
- Type 2 diabetes
- High blood pressure
- Heart disease
- Stroke

Healthy eating and regular physical activity help prevent overnutrition.

---

# Obesity

Obesity is excessive body fat that may increase the risk of disease.

Risk factors include:

- High-calorie diets
- Physical inactivity
- Family history
- Poor sleep
- Certain medical conditions

Maintaining a healthy weight reduces the risk of many chronic diseases.

---

# Nutrition and Chronic Diseases

Healthy eating helps reduce the risk of many chronic diseases.

### High Blood Pressure

People with hypertension should:

- Reduce salt intake
- Eat more fruits and vegetables
- Choose whole grains
- Maintain a healthy weight

---

### Diabetes

People with diabetes should:

- Monitor carbohydrate intake
- Eat regular balanced meals
- Limit sugary foods and drinks
- Increase dietary fibre
- Follow healthcare providers' advice

---

### Heart Disease

Heart-healthy eating includes:

- Fruits
- Vegetables
- Whole grains
- Fish
- Beans
- Healthy oils

Limit foods high in saturated fat, trans fat, and salt.

# Food Hygiene and Food Safety

Safe food preparation reduces foodborne illness.

Recommendations include:

- Wash hands before preparing food.
- Wash fruits and vegetables thoroughly.
- Cook meat completely.
- Drink safe, clean water.
- Refrigerate perishable foods promptly.
- Avoid expired foods.
- Prevent cross-contamination between raw and cooked foods.
- Keep kitchen surfaces clean.

Good food hygiene protects the entire family from infections.

# Nutrition Myths and Facts

Myth

Healthy food is always expensive.

Fact: Many healthy foods such as beans, local grains, vegetables, and seasonal fruits can be affordable.

Myth

Skipping meals helps with weight loss.

Fact: Skipping meals may lead to overeating later and does not guarantee healthy weight loss.

Myth

Children need sugary drinks to grow well.

Fact: Children grow best with nutritious foods and clean water rather than sugary drinks.

Myth

People with diabetes cannot eat fruit.

Fact: Most people with diabetes can eat fruit in appropriate portions as part of a balanced diet.

# Frequently Asked Questions

## What is a balanced diet?

A balanced diet contains appropriate amounts of carbohydrates, proteins, healthy fats, vitamins, minerals, fibre, and water.

## How much water should I drink?

Water needs vary by age, activity level, climate, and health status. Most people should drink enough fluids to stay well hydrated.

## Are supplements necessary?

Some people may need supplements such as iron, folic acid, calcium, or vitamin D. Supplements should be taken based on medical advice.

## What foods help prevent high blood pressure?

Fruits, vegetables, whole grains, beans, and foods low in salt can support healthy blood pressure.

## What foods are rich in iron?

Lean meat, liver, beans, spinach, and fortified cereals are good sources of iron.

# Key Points

Good nutrition supports health at every stage of life.
