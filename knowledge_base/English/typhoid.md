# Typhoid Fever

## Overview

Typhoid fever is a serious bacterial infection caused by *Salmonella enterica* serotype Typhi (*Salmonella Typhi*). It spreads mainly through contaminated food and water and is common in areas with poor sanitation and limited access to clean drinking water.

Typhoid fever can affect people of all ages. Without proper treatment, the infection may lead to severe complications such as intestinal bleeding or perforation, which can be life-threatening.

Early diagnosis and prompt treatment significantly improve recovery and reduce complications.

---

# Causes

Typhoid fever is caused by the bacterium *Salmonella Typhi*.

People become infected by:

- Drinking contaminated water
- Eating contaminated food
- Poor hand hygiene
- Close contact with an infected person
- Eating food prepared by an infected food handler
- Poor sanitation

The bacteria enter the digestive system and spread through the bloodstream to various organs.

---

# Risk Factors

Risk factors include:

- Drinking untreated water
- Living in areas with poor sanitation
- Poor personal hygiene
- Traveling to regions where typhoid is common
- Eating food from unhygienic vendors
- Floods or humanitarian crises
- Weak immune system

Children and young adults are commonly affected.

---

# Signs and Symptoms

Symptoms usually appear **6 to 30 days** after exposure.

Common symptoms include:

- Persistent high fever
- Headache
- Weakness
- Fatigue
- Loss of appetite
- Abdominal pain
- Constipation
- Diarrhea
- Nausea
- Vomiting
- Dry cough
- Muscle aches

Some people may develop a pink-colored rash ("rose spots") on the chest or abdomen.

---

# Severe Symptoms

Seek urgent medical attention if the patient develops:

- Persistent high fever
- Severe abdominal pain
- Bloody stool
- Severe diarrhea
- Confusion
- Difficulty staying awake
- Severe weakness
- Persistent vomiting
- Abdominal swelling
- Difficulty breathing
- Loss of consciousness

These may indicate serious complications.

---

# Possible Complications

Untreated typhoid fever may cause:

- Severe dehydration
- Intestinal bleeding
- Intestinal perforation
- Bloodstream infection (sepsis)
- Pneumonia
- Kidney infection
- Meningitis (rare)
- Shock
- Death

Prompt treatment greatly reduces these risks.

---

# Diagnosis

Healthcare providers may diagnose typhoid using:

- Medical history
- Physical examination
- Blood culture
- Stool culture
- Urine culture
- Bone marrow culture (special cases)
- Complete blood count (CBC)

Laboratory confirmation is important because symptoms may resemble malaria or other infections.

---

# Treatment

Treatment should always be supervised by a healthcare professional.

Treatment may include:

- Appropriate antibiotics
- Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS) if diarrhea is present
- Intravenous fluids for severe dehydration
- Fever-reducing medication when appropriate
- Adequate nutrition
- Plenty of rest

Complete the full course of prescribed antibiotics even if symptoms improve.

---

# Home Care

During recovery:

- Drink plenty of safe fluids.
- Eat soft, nutritious meals.
- Rest adequately.
- Wash hands regularly.
- Avoid preparing food for others while sick.
- Take medications exactly as prescribed.
- Return for follow-up if symptoms worsen.

---

# Prevention

Typhoid fever can be prevented by:

- Drinking safe treated water
- Washing hands with soap
- Using proper sanitation
- Washing fruits and vegetables
- Cooking food thoroughly
- Avoiding raw or undercooked foods
- Avoiding unsafe street food
- Receiving typhoid vaccination when recommended
- Practicing good food hygiene

Community sanitation plays a major role in prevention.

---

# Nutrition During Recovery

Recovery is supported by:

- Plenty of clean water
- ORS when needed
- Soft foods
- Fruits
- Vegetables
- Rice
- Beans
- Eggs
- Fish
- Lean meat

Avoid alcohol and heavily contaminated foods.

---

# When to Seek Emergency Care

Go to the nearest hospital immediately if the patient has:

- Severe abdominal pain
- Persistent high fever
- Bloody stool
- Severe dehydration
- Confusion
- Difficulty breathing
- Persistent vomiting
- Unable to drink fluids
- Seizures
- Loss of consciousness

Emergency treatment may save life.

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Is typhoid contagious?

Typhoid spreads through contaminated food and water. It is not spread by casual contact such as shaking hands, but poor hygiene can contribute to transmission.

---

## Can typhoid be cured?

Yes. Most patients recover completely with early diagnosis and appropriate antibiotic treatment.

---

## Is typhoid the same as malaria?

No.

Typhoid fever is caused by bacteria (*Salmonella Typhi*), while malaria is caused by *Plasmodium* parasites transmitted by infected mosquitoes.

---

## Can someone get typhoid more than once?

Yes. Previous infection does not always provide lifelong immunity.

---

## Is vaccination available?

Yes. Typhoid vaccines are available and may be recommended for people traveling to or living in high-risk areas.

---

# Key Points

- Typhoid fever is caused by *Salmonella Typhi*.
- It spreads through contaminated food and water.
- Persistent high fever is the most common symptom.
- Early diagnosis improves recovery.
- Antibiotics are the main treatment.
- Safe water, sanitation, and hygiene prevent infection.
- Vaccination provides additional protection in high-risk settings.
