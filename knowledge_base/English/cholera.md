# Cholera

## Overview

Cholera is an acute diarrheal disease caused by the bacterium *Vibrio cholerae*. The infection is usually acquired by drinking contaminated water or eating contaminated food. Cholera can rapidly lead to severe dehydration because of excessive loss of water and salts through diarrhea and vomiting.

Although cholera can affect people of all ages, it is more common in areas with poor sanitation, inadequate clean water supply, overcrowding, and poor hygiene. If untreated, severe cholera can become life-threatening within a few hours.

The disease is preventable through safe drinking water, proper sanitation, good hygiene practices, and vaccination in high-risk areas.

---

# Causes

Cholera is caused by infection with the bacterium **Vibrio cholerae**.

People become infected after consuming:

- Contaminated drinking water
- Contaminated food
- Raw or undercooked seafood
- Food prepared under poor hygienic conditions
- Fruits or vegetables washed with contaminated water

The bacteria produce toxins inside the intestines, causing the body to release large amounts of water into the digestive tract.

---

# Risk Factors

The risk of cholera increases in people who:

- Drink untreated water
- Live in overcrowded communities
- Have poor sanitation facilities
- Practice poor hand hygiene
- Eat food from unhygienic sources
- Live in areas experiencing floods or conflicts
- Travel to regions with cholera outbreaks
- Have reduced stomach acid

Children, older adults, and people with weakened immune systems are more vulnerable to severe illness.

---

# Signs and Symptoms

Symptoms usually appear between **12 hours and 5 days** after infection.

Common symptoms include:

- Sudden onset of watery diarrhea
- Profuse "rice-water" stools
- Vomiting
- Extreme thirst
- Dry mouth
- Muscle cramps
- Weakness
- Fatigue
- Sunken eyes
- Dry skin
- Rapid heartbeat
- Reduced urination

Many infected individuals have mild symptoms or no symptoms at all but can still spread the disease.

---

# Severe Symptoms

Severe cholera causes rapid dehydration.

Danger signs include:

- Severe dehydration
- Very little or no urine
- Cold hands and feet
- Fast weak pulse
- Confusion
- Drowsiness
- Low blood pressure
- Loss of consciousness
- Shock

Severe dehydration is a medical emergency.

---

# Diagnosis

Healthcare providers may diagnose cholera using:

- Medical history
- Physical examination
- Stool sample testing
- Rapid cholera diagnostic tests
- Laboratory culture of stool

Diagnosis is especially important during outbreaks.

---

# Treatment

Treatment should begin immediately.

The primary treatment is replacing lost fluids and salts.

Recommended treatments include:

- Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS)
- Zinc supplements for children (where recommended)
- Intravenous (IV) fluids for severe dehydration
- Antibiotics only when prescribed by healthcare professionals
- Continued feeding during recovery

Early treatment greatly reduces the risk of death.

---

# Home Care

For mild illness:

- Begin ORS immediately
- Drink plenty of safe clean water
- Continue eating small nutritious meals
- Wash hands frequently
- Avoid sugary soft drinks instead of ORS
- Monitor for worsening symptoms

Never delay seeking medical care if dehydration develops.

---

# Prevention

Cholera can be prevented through:

- Drinking safe treated water
- Boiling water when necessary
- Washing hands with soap
- Using proper toilet facilities
- Proper disposal of waste
- Washing fruits and vegetables thoroughly
- Cooking food completely
- Eating food while it is still hot
- Avoiding unsafe street food during outbreaks
- Oral cholera vaccination in high-risk settings

Community sanitation is one of the most effective prevention measures.

---

# Possible Complications

Without treatment, cholera may lead to:

- Severe dehydration
- Electrolyte imbalance
- Kidney failure
- Low blood pressure
- Shock
- Seizures (rare)
- Death

Prompt treatment prevents most complications.

---

# When to Seek Emergency Care

Seek immediate medical attention if the patient develops:

- Severe watery diarrhea
- Persistent vomiting
- Signs of dehydration
- Unable to drink fluids
- Very little urine
- Confusion
- Loss of consciousness
- Severe weakness
- Rapid breathing
- Shock

Children and older adults should receive medical attention early.

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Is cholera contagious?

Cholera spreads through contaminated food and water rather than casual person-to-person contact.

---

## Can cholera be cured?

Yes. With early treatment and proper rehydration, most patients recover completely.

---

## Can ORS save lives?

Yes. Oral Rehydration Solution is one of the most effective treatments for preventing dehydration caused by cholera.

---

## Can cholera be prevented?

Yes. Safe drinking water, sanitation, handwashing, food hygiene, and vaccination help prevent cholera.

---

## Can children get cholera?

Yes. Children are particularly vulnerable to dehydration and should receive prompt treatment.

---

# Key Points

- Cholera is caused by *Vibrio cholerae*.
- It spreads mainly through contaminated water and food.
- Severe watery diarrhea is the main symptom.
- Rapid dehydration is the greatest danger.
- ORS should be started immediately.
- Severe cases require hospital treatment.
- Good sanitation and clean water prevent cholera.
